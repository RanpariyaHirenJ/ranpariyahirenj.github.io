Juice cleansing
What all you need to know

Looking at the lifestyle today, we know how health has gone for a toss. Stress, lack of physical activity and junk "snacks" are getting stacked in our routines. Health has now become just a coffee table talk! In this quest to reset our mind, body and soul, we desperately long for healthy diets accompanied with new fangled appliances. Juice cleansing is the first step while reestablishing your connection to a healthy diet.

Are you familiar with that feeling of bloating and sluggishness, when you feel tired whilst craving for junk food and caffeine? Feelings like these are synonymous when toxins have crept into your body without your knowledge. Eliminating toxins from your system, makes your entire body feel better both internally and externally. This is where "The juice cleanse" comes in to the picture. Read on to know what is it all about.

Intake of Aloe Vera juice

The words basically give it away. Cleansing your body with juices can be held as a literal meaning. A juice cleanse is basically done with the purpose of detoxifying the digestive system. The body is exposed to various factors, which result in oxidative stress and depletion of antioxidants. So, cleansing your body becomes critical. A juice cleanse is based on the fact that the process of detoxification in the body requires a wide range of vitamins and minerals along with fluids. Thus juices are the best candidate in this case.

Many raw fruits and vegetables contain excellent cleansing properties, especially green vegetables which are very good for healing the liver and the digestive tract.

Aim of juice cleansing

  • Boost effectiveness of the digestive system.
  • Stimulating other parts of the body which are responsible for cleansing and detoxifying.
  • To shift away the focus from bad habits like too much coffee, alcohol and fatty foods.
  • To help foster healthier habits.
  • To shed the water weight from the body.

How it's done?

Where on one side the internet is flooded with different types of juice cleanses, the best way is to first consult your doctor or a proper nutritionist. There is no rocket science in doing juice cleanses. The only thing you take care is to consume enough juices to keep you on your feet.

Avoid solid food like cereals and pulses. The only solid things you can eat includes cut fruits and raw salads

Directly jumping onto the fad will do you more bad than good. A juice cleanse should be done for no more than 7 days in a row. Although a nutritionist will be able to tell you better, you can go ahead with smaller intervals like 3 days.

Pros of Juice cleansing:

  • A juice cleanse eliminates wheat, dairy, gluten and fermented foods, from your diet. Reintroducing your body to them after the cleanse can help define what your body is not agreeing to.
  • As you consume low calories, the body uses up the glycogen stores and you will lose your water weight.
  • Keeping yourself away from food may help calm down your appetite and eliminate the need to eat for comfort.
  • Juices are an easy way to get a quick vitamin and nutrient boost, as in the liquid form it is easier for the body to absorb these.

Cons of Juice cleansing:

  • The metabolism slows down as without food the body goes into energy conservation mode.
  • As the glycogen stored in muscles is used up, your skin may feel dry. And prolonged juice cleanses may lead to premature ageing.
  • If you are only consuming juices, the body does not get protein and so you may lose muscle mass.
  • Juice cleanses lead to diarrhea, as the liquid will pass through the digestive tract much faster.

Just like other trends, this one is here to stay as people continue to give mixed reviews for the same. But trying out never harmed anyone. So, if you plan to give it a try, make sure you give your nutritionist a call asking him everything you can about healthy drink juices and keep in mind the above points.
