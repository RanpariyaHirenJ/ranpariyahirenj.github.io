Experience Design

Our likes and dislikes in every aspect of our lives are based on the experiences we’ve had with that particular phase or product. Almost everything is an experience isn’t it? and perhaps in ways interactive as well. What makes you like something is how it made you feel.

‘Experience is the teacher of all things’ said Julius Ceaser in the 100 BC. Fast forward to the present and we have Denise Lee Yawn saying – ‘Customer Experience and employee experience are now two of the driving forces of business’. Not much has changed. The context yes. But quintessentially it remains the same.

So, what is Experience Design and why is it so important, especially in today’s digitally dependent world? In spirit, Experience Design is an empathetic song. It extracts tenors and pitches from Psychology, Sociology, Technology, Cultural & Genetic anthropology and many more such domains. It creates a tune that’s perfect and melodious for all those who hear it.

There’s no definitive list of to-dos and must-haves here. It is a way of thinking that’s case specific. Its designing keeping the user’s viewpoint in mind and creating great customer experience. It is not a substitute for a good product or can it supersede brand strategy. Rather it complements the innovation and enhances its value. It showcases your concepts and adds meaning to the product.

Experience Design is a method- to enrich the mundane, to turn the complex into simple, to titillate your intellect. It’s reaching out and meeting the user’s needs and doing it flawlessly. It’s a conversation between a consumer and the product, and how the conversation makes the user feel. It’s not about the technology or interface that’s used, its simply the meaningful experience that has been created. It’s human centric.

Experience Design focusses on the ‘why’ of the product. There are many tools & methods used by designers to highlight this why and create that perfect story. It’s what we at DQ have incorporated time and again.

Investigate & Service Blueprint

An in-depth design research that’s distributed over multiple phases to recognize the requirements, inspirations and behaviours of the populace they design for, and how the product would be of use to them. It helps creating the wireframes & service blueprint and paves the way ahead by creating the context of the design.

Decipher & Creating Personas

A lot of designers create surveys and interviews as well which are catalogued and used while designing. It’s a great way to create a persona of the user and make it more human centric. Creating fictional profiles helps decipher real consumer needs. It helps narrow down the target audience, which aids in the eventual success of the product.

Task Flows & User Journeys

Another great tool is creating the master flows. Task Flows & User journeys help in investigating what currently exists by creating a prognosis of the existing service, or they can help perceive a new product by creating a fabricated journey. There are many mediums to do this- Graphical representations, write ups, slideshows, videos. There are many possibilities to it.

Workshops & Co-creating sessions

Workshops are a great way to bring diversity and mold thoughts together. It can help generate innovative solutions. Many designers, us included, create group exercises to reach a specific outcome. There’s always more to gain, new ideas and new thinking approaches are formed, when you co-create and work together towards a common solution. It’s also a great tool to get your team to think out-of-the box and make it a norm in your own company.

Prototype

By definition prototype is an initial or preliminary version from which other forms are developed. It’s what gives you perception into the functionality of your design and the changes that need to be incorporated to make it better. The objective of creating prototypes is to run tests, collect feedback and incorporate those pointers into the next prototype. It’s an ongoing repetitive cycle of creating and testing.

What we’ve listed are what we at DQ have been following and what we deem important in creating great User Experience for the products we design. Many others would have a different strategy and approach, which might differ in the terminology but the roots remain the same.
