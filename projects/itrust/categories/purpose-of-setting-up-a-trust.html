Purpose of setting up a trust

Posted on September 17, 2013 by Rizwan Inayat

Trusts are generally set up for investment, business or estate planning purposes. Family Trusts are also set up for asset protection purposes and a great vehicle for minimising taxes and building wealth. Each scenario will need to be looked at separately before determining whether a trust is the right structure to be in. Just like with any other structure out there a trust needs to be tailored to suit your individual needs.

Advantages of setting up a family or discretionary trust.

Some of the advantages of setting up a family or discretionary trust are:

  • Being able to distribute income to beneficiaries on low income or lower tax brackets;
  • Asset protection advantages if set up correctly;
  • Access to capital gains concessions which aren't available in company structures;
  • Estate planning benefits;
  • This structure allows you to do some advanced tax planning and is a great mechanism to minimising taxes.

Disadvantages of setting up a family trust or discretionary trust

Some of the disadvantages of setting up a family or discretionary trust are:

  • Inability to retain profits at the company tax rate of 30%
  • Cash flow disadvantages for negatively geared property; and
  • Complexity involved in setting it up and maintaining it on an ongoing basis if you do it yourself

The process of setting up a trust:

  • Initial step is to come in for a complimentary meeting to meet with one of our trust specialists who will assess your needs and advise whether a trust is a suitable structure for yourself.
  • Once we have determined that a trust is a suitable structure we can go through a few different options and scenarios on setting up a trust and advise on the most suitable trust structure.
  • Our lawyers will than prepare the trust deed tailored to suit your needs.
  • Our accountants will than Prepare and lodge ABN and TFN applications for the trust and any other registrations required with the ATO.
  • A second meeting will than be arranged to go through all the documentation and sign off on all the minutes.
  • A bank account will need to be set up for the trust and the deed will need to be stamped with the Office of State Revenue which we can arrange for you.
  • We will provide you with ongoing advice as and when required on the day to day running of a trust.
