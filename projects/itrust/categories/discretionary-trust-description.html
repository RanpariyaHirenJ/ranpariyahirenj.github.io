Individual Trustee or Corporate Trustee for a Trust?

Posted on October 16, 2013 by Rizwan Inayat

The trustee of a trust has legal control over the assets of a trust and is involved in the day to day decision making i.e how to distribute income, assets to purchase etc. A critical step in any type of trust structure is determining whether the trustee should be an individual or a corporate entity. Choosing between an individual trustee vs corporate trustee will determine the level of asset protection you have.

For asset protection purposes a corporate trustee is usually preferred over an individual trustee. Since the trustee is the legal owner of the asset any lawsuits against the trust will expose the trustee. So if the trustee is an individual their personal assets are also exposed i.e. the family home. However, if the trustee is a company the lawsuit or actions will be limited to the assets of the company. It is generally recommended that the corporate trustee be a non trading entity with no assets otherwise it will be exposing the assets it holds. Being a non-trading entity also means that the company will not be required to lodge a tax return hence reducing on going compliance costs.

Apart from asset protection a corporate trustee has numerous estate planning benefits and can save thousands in administration costs moving forward as Changing the director of a trustee company is a lot easier than changing the trustee all together.

Although a corporate trustee provides a lot more asset protection than an individual trustee structuring trusts for asset protections can be a complicated exercise and specialist advice should be sought.
