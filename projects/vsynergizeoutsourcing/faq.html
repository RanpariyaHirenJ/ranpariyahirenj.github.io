What is the difference between Lead Generation and Demand Generation

Demand Generation and Lead Generation are both highly important for B2B Marketing Strategies. Demand Generation is the method of acquiring the interest of the audience in what you have to sell i.e. creating demand; Lead Generation is the process of turning that procured interest into the user details i.e. Leads that you or your sales teams can follow up with. The very noticeable difference between a Demand Generation Campaign and a Lead Generation Campaign is therefore whether the campaign asks for contact information or not. If you have your content behind a form asking for contact details and distribute it to a target audience, then you are running a lead generation campaign. On the other hand, if you make content freely accessible, and then pay to distribute it to as many relevant people as you can, then you are in the business of generating demand.

What is the difference between B2B Marketing and B2C Marketing?

B2B is an acronym for Business to Business, as the name describes, it is a type of commercial transaction where the purchasing and selling of merchandise are performed between two businesses, such as entity supplying material to another for production, or entity providing services to another. B2C, where the business sells its goods and services to the final consumer. Those companies whose products and services are employed directly by the end-user are known as B2C companies. There are many major differences between B2B and B2C, which you can see in the article below. In B2B Market buying, decisions are completely Planned and Logical, based on needs. In B2C Market buying decisions are Emotional and based on want and desire of consumers. You'll also notice that a lot of times branding for B2C is a lot more colorful and quirky then branding for B2B counterparts. Trust and Mutual Relationship in B2B Market creates the Brand Value, whereas Advertising and Promotion helps B2C Market create its Brand Value.
