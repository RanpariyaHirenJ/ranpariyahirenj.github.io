Why Branding?

Why invest in Brand Design and Branding? No, we’re not here to tell you to hire us (oh well! maybe a little bit...!) But we really do want to tell you why you need it.

Strange and uncertain times these are, and strange and uncertain circumstances we see. A faceless invisible virus keeping us all locked up inside with our views shut. What are you left with then? Quite a bit to be honest. The incredible world of cyber technology has connected us digitally while being physically distanced. And we’ve evolved as we always do. We’ve adapted to the current scenario and made the most of it. Brain storming over video calls to online presentations- we’ve learnt it all.

While the future looks uncertain and we don’t know when the world would heal, we do know that the way we work has changed and keeping up with these changing times is the need of the hour. How do we keep up though? we change our tactics and in the word of digital interactions, we build our brand.

Think of Branding as your story. You choose your plot and the characters. You define what they look like and how they make you feel. You design the cover and select the text and how the story ends. It can’t be that simple you would think. It is! we say. No really, it is!

First thing first- Branding isn’t just your logo. It’s the entire customer experience. It means that your logo, website / apps, social media to letter head templates, business cards- all follow the same uniformity and design.

Branding develops your who

When there’s uniformity & consistency in style and design it creates a connection, which subconsciously registers in the mind and you tend to identify a brand. It’s called the strategy of remembrance. It basically is your business’s personality and is what shapes your business. It adds character into your work and just as you have your personal identity which makes you-you, branding carves a niche for your business / product.

Branding creates connections & bonds

What’s this connection we mentioned earlier? Well, once you establish yourself as a brand & create your identity, you connect with consumers, workers and the general public. This connection takes time and it’s an on-going process, but it commences by creating a good reputation, letting your customers get to know you and eventually finding significant ways to connect. Branding unites an audience. Connection on a profound, individual level is an indication of a powerful brand.

Branding makes happy employees happier.

Let’s face it, everyone loves to gloat about working with a top-notch brand. In our society, a lot of value is given to who works where, and working with a well-known and established brand boosts a person’s self-esteem in not just in the eyes of others but his own. It’s a feel-good factor, which in turn creates loyalty & great company culture and employee morale verves right with it.

Branding makes you the talk of the town.

People love to talk. And people love to show off. And people love to speak about brands. People dress in brands, consume brands, hear about brands, attend brand shows & talks, and they’re endlessly telling others about the brands they love. Now you can’t tell someone about a brand you can’t recall, can you? Haah! there’s branding for you!

Your brand makes you dazzle brightest amongst the bright.

The word Brand has been used and overused and has become synonymous with all things luxurious. But really…The legal term for brand is trademark. What Branding implies in the world of digital media is your personal unique identity. It is defining your persona; it adds character and that oomph factor which is so imperative for product differentiation. It is what will make you shine bright in this saturated market.

Brand makes you the go-to

Branding is not just about attaining consumers to select you over others. It's also about making the consumers see you as the ultimate solution provider for their wants and desires. It will motivate the buyer to make that purchase and look to you for doing it. In its crux, branding is a solution specialist.

We’ve listed just six of the many reasons to opt for branding, we could go on and on about it. Fundamentally it is the gist of who you are and what you can do for your consumers. When you can sum up that up without obviously voicing it – you’ve built your brand.

We’ll sign off by quoting Jeff Bezos, CEO of Amazon, who probably sums it all up in one sentence:

“Your brand is what other people say about you when you’re not in the room.”


Thanks for reading!
