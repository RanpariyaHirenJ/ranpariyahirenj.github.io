Everyone loves great deals and savings. So why not try to get the lowest rates on hotels? Here are a few tips to get an advantage while booking a hotel room.

10 Tips to Save on Hotels

  • Whenever possible, book your room in advance. Especially when it comes to booking a vacation at hotspots which see rate spikes during season. Places like Myrtle Beach see a high rate during the summer months. Booking during the peak season usually means paying a higher rate. However, you may be able to get lower rates if you book in advance
  • Call the property direct. Sometimes, the rates you see online may not be the best rate offered. The hotel may have had 5 last minute cancellations and would be willing to offer those 5 rooms for a lower rate. By calling the hotel directly, you get an opportunity to see what lowest rate they offer. Why leave the chance to save?
  • If you can, book rooms on low demand week days. Rates are usually higher on the weekends. And, if your travel needs a weekend stay, book well in advance to get lower rates.
  • Try price matching. This is more applicable for walk-in reservations. Find the lowest price online and ask the hotel if they can price match with online rates.
  • Long stay discouunts. If you're planning to stay for a week or more, ask for the hotel's weekly rate. Most hotels offer discounts on weekly and monthly rates.
  • Use all available discount options like a coupon, AAA, AARP, senior, military, group and corporate. For example, your employer may have a special rate package with the hotel. Always make sure to use all your discount options.
  • Hotels are constantly trying to improve their online review ratings. Some hotels will offer you discounts if you willingly agree to write them a review. Always ask the hotel staff and see if they are willing to lower your rate in return for a review. Or, if you are social media savvy, offer to share their hotel link with your followers on Facebook or Twitter. This sometimes works!
  • Early check out - Looking to get a room for just a few hours? Ask the hotel if they will give you a lower rate for an early checkout.
  • Membership rewards - If you aren't already a member of the hotel group, try signing up before booking a room. Many hotels will send you a special discount code in a few minutes of signing up. You can always use this to lower your rate. If not, you still will earn points for your stay which can later add up to free stays or deep discount rates.
  • Group discounts - If you are a large group booking 3-4 rooms, make sure to ask the hotel for a group discount. Most hotels offer group booking discounts. Don't lose out on this opportunity.
