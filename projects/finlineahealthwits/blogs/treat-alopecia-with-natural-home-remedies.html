Treat Alopecia With Natural Home Remedies

Alopecia is a broad and general medical term for all kinds of hair loss and affects more than 1 million cases per year in India. Not all hair loss is of concern. Losing 50 and 100 strands per day is considered normal, based on the average scalp comprising of 100,000 hair follicles. For those who undergo more than this, hair loss or thinning usually takes place across the top of the head, so can be hard to blot out. In these cases, one may often find the change in their appearance understandably upsetting. Alopecia does not only affect the hair on our head. A lot of people who suffer from alopecia lose hair from eyebrows, eyelashes and even arm or leg hair depending upon the type of hair loss with varied symptoms and causes.

  • Causes of Alopecia

    Causes of alopecia vary and those causes further indicate which type of alopecia you have. The causes may include-
    • Hereditary genetics
    • Emotional and physical stress
    • Hormonal changes
    • Autoimmune diseases such as thyroid disease or vitiligo
    • Crash dieting and strong medication etc.

  • Types of Alopecia

    Different types of alopecia are as follows-
    • Alopecia Areata (AA) :- Alopecia or hair loss occurs in patches anywhere on the body. This type of Alopecia is an immune system disorder.
    • Alopecia Barbae :- Alopecia or hair loss occurs when men experience facial hair loss, predominantly in the beard area.
    • Alopecia Mucinosa

  • This type of Alopecia results in scaly patches.

    • Androgenetic Alopecia (AGA)

      Alopecia or hair loss is usually labeled as Male pattern baldness, but it occurs to women as well. It is the hair thinning to the point where it is almost transparent. This form of alopecia is said to be caused mainly due to genetic disposition.

    • Alopecia Totalis (AT)

      Complete hair loss on the scalp.

    • Alopecia Universalis (AU)

      Complete loss of hair on the scalp and body.

    • Scarring Alopecia

      The loss of hair which is accompanied with scarring.

    • Traction Alopecia

      Traction Alopecia is caused by excessive pulling or tension on the hair shafts. This type of hair loss is quite evident in women. Increased tension on the hair shaft can stop new hair follicles from developing and may lead to permanent hair loss.

Home Remedies for Alopecia

Some of the home remedies that can help you deal with Alopecia are-

  • Garlic

    • Garlic contains a large amount of sulfur and it is one of the best home remedies for alopecia. The nutrients from the garlic boost the production of collagen, which can increase hair growth.

    How to use it-

    Step 1: Pick some garlic cloves.

    Step 2: Crushed to make juice.

    Step 3: Add some spoons of coconut oil.

    Step 4: Put the mixture to heat for a few minutes.

    Step 5: Once it cools down, apply it over your scalp or massage well.

    Step 6: After about half an hour, use cold water to wash the hair.

  • Aloe Vera Juice

    • Aloe Vera, undoubtedly, is extremely beneficial for the skin and hair. In the treatment of alopecia, it increases the growth of new hair and stops the hair loss at the same time. Freshly extracted gel from Aloe Vera or Aloe Vera juice should be used to get the best results.

    How to use it-

    Step 1: Rub it over your scalp and hair. Then, massage those areas.

    Step 2: After some time, wash your hair with cold water.

    You should follow this remedy 2-3 times (every day) to get rid of alopecia naturally in the least amount of time.

  • Coconut Oil

    • It has been considered as one of the best home remedies for alopecia since years and is very simple to use as well.

    How to use it-

    Step 1: Put it directly on your hair before going to bed at night.

    Step 2: Wash your hair with water next morning.

  • Mustard Oil

    • Mustard oil is also very effective in treating baldness.

    How to use it-

    Step 1: Boil some mustard oil.

    Step 2: Add about 4 spoons of crushed henna leaves and boil for a while.

    Step 3: Strain the mixture and let it cool.

    Step 4: Once it cools down, apply it on your hair.

  • Shampoo With Tea

    • This is among one of the most effective and cheapest ways to treat alopecia.

    How to use it-

    Step 1: Mix 50 g of tea with one liter of warm water.

    Step 2: Use it as a natural shampoo to apply after your usual shampoo.

  • Egg

    • To achieve a beautiful and healthy skin, the egg should be included in your daily meals. But it is also a great natural remedy for alopecia as well. A diversity of essential nutrients can be found in an egg, such as sulfur, protein, iodine or selenium. They all play a significant role in the growth of healthy hair.

    How to use it-

    Step 1: Take egg white.

    Step 2: Add olive oil and mix them together.

    Step 3: Apply the paste over your hair.

    Step 4: After 15-20 minutes, use cool water and shampoo to wash the hair.

  • Oatmeal

    • Oatmeal has an abundance of fiber, which works effectively to prevent hair loss. In addition, omega-3 fatty acids from an oatmeal can bring down the number of falling hair. Thus, oatmeal must be included in your daily meals to treat alopecia.

  • Hibiscus

    • Hibiscus nourishes hair, prevents early graying, treats dandruff and controls hair fall.

    How to use it-

    Step 1: Crush a few flowers and mix coconut or sesame oil to make a paste.

    Step 2: Put on the scalp and hair and let it stay on for a few hours.

    Step 3: Rinse off with cold water using a mild shampoo.

    Step 4: After 15-20 minutes, use cool water and shampoo to wash the hair.
