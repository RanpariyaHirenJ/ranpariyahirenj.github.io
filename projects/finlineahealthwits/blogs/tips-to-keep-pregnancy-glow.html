Tips to keep that
pregnancy glow

It is said that pregnancy gives your face a natural glow, but that's not the complete truth. With pregnancy comes skin issues like dull spots, excessive oiliness and unwanted blemishes. Many women stop taking care of their skin during pregnancy, but pregnancy doesn't mean halting your beauty regime. A little amount of clever application and you're set for the day. Set a minimal skin care routine for every day and you'll actually get a baby-is-coming glow.

Few of the makeup tips are

  • Cleanse your Skin

    Cleansing your skin twice a day is a complete must. But during pregnancy, you need to be careful about what kind of ingredients your cleanser contains. Switch to a mild, alcohol-free and oil-free cleanser for the time being and avoid cleansers that contain salicylic acid or beta hydroxy acids.

  • Change your products according to your pregnancy skin

    During pregnancy, your skin may become more oily or dry as compared to your normal skin. Therefore, use rich moisturizers and other makeup products that provide moisture to the skin, if it becomes too dry. If your skin becomes too oily, then avoid using oil-based products. Instead, go for powder-based which are oil absorbing and will help minimize the clogging of pores. Avoid moisturizers that contain retinoids as high doses could be harmful to your unborn baby.

  • Acne Minimization and Coverage

    Acne may show up when you are pregnant and it is common during the first and second trimesters. Do not over-wash your face and avoid popping, scratching or picking acne. Do not take any medications without consulting your doctor. Avoid using a product with salicylic acid ingredients. Keep your hands off your face as there are bacteria on the fingers.

  • Sunscreen

    This again is another must have as your skin might get sensitive during pregnancy. Get a natural sunscreen with a higher SPF value, and apply it every day, even if you have to step out for just a little while.

  • Go Light on Foundation

    You don't want a very heavy foundation sitting on your skin all day when you already feel bloated. Switch to BB creams or tinted moisturizers. They'll provide fair enough coverage and feel completely light on the skin.

  • Concealer

    Sometimes, due to lack of proper sleep and tiredness, your eyes may look puffy and those dark circles might pile on. Use a night eye cream and serum daily to give your eyes proper treatment. If you have to attend an event, then you can use a concealer of the right shade to cover the under eye dark circles.

  • Go less

    The key to a natural glow during pregnancy is to not go overboard with makeup. Avoid using colours that are too tacky or bright.

  • Carry a facial mist or spritz

    Whenever you want to freshen up your face, spritz some facial mist and you'll get a less puffy and a refreshed look.

  • Sleep

    Take enough rest and sleep to beat the stress of increasing weight and hormonal changes. Keep your posture right and make yourself completely comfortable when you go to sleep.

  • Exercise

    Exercise is really important during pregnancy. You can join various prenatal exercise or yoga classes in order to gain strength and flexibility. Also, it prevents you from gaining weight rapidly, which is the primary cause of stretch marks.

  • Eat right

    What you eat or drink during your pregnancy is your baby's main source of nourishment. So, include protein, iron, folic acid and calcium in your diet. Remember to take your prenatal vitamins daily. Include fresh fruit and vegetable juices in your diet for glowing skin.

  • Drink lots of water

    Make sure that you drink at least 2 litres of water every day. It does not just help in flushing out toxins from the body, but also maintain the right amount of amniotic fluid in your body.

Just go with a natural, subtle and glowing look. A little bit of makeup and a lovely maternal glow will make you look like a beautiful mother-to-be!
