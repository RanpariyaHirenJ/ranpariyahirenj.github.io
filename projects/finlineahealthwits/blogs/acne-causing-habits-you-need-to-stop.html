Acne Causing Habits You Need to Stop Right NOW!

I cleanse, moisturize, tone, exfoliate and use sunblock, then why the hell am I still getting acne? There is a plethora of reasons to get acne. It is the most annoying skin problem to get as it ruins the appearance of your face, something you and everybody around you get to see all the time. Most of us spend a lot of time trying to deceive acne by frequent dermatologist visits, acne creams, OTC medications and skin care regime, but still, it somehow manages to fool us. Talk about frustration! In the good old days, it was easier to manage acne but today, it’s a whole different ball game. We have become so immune to living in polluted and filthy areas and leading unhealthy lifestyles. All of this takes a toll on different parts of our body without us even realizing it. So, what can you do to prevent yourself from getting acne and taking better care of your skin?

  • Get Enough Sleep

    When you get enough sleep, you lower the stress levels of your body and put yourself at ease. Research has shown that at least 7 hours of sleep is mandatory for the body to perform at optimum levels. If you have high-stress levels and an un-energized body, it will show on your face. So, time to follow the ‘early to bed and early to rise makes a man/woman healthy, wealthy and wise’ routine.

  • Stop Touching Your Face Too Often

    Dermatologists will always suggest keeping your hands away from your face for the most part of the day. There is obviously a lot of logic that goes into that statement. Your hands come in contact with a lot of pollutants and bacteria through the day, which can lead to further breakouts on your face. Also, keep antibacterial wipes for your cell phone as dirt from your fingers can build up on it, giving you breakouts around your cheeks.

  • Applying Your Body Cream on Your Face is a Big NO

    Body creams are meant for your body and face creams are made for your face. Creams that are designed for your body tend to be much stronger, whereas the ones designed for your face are milder. Using a strong cream on your face will cause burning, rashes and even breakouts as they may clog the sensitive pores on your face.

  • Do Not Change Skin Products Too Often

    Not sure which cream suits you better, so you try to purchase them all and use them as and when you feel? Maybe your skin doesn’t feel so good about that. You should consult your dermatologist before buying or using any skin care products that you find at pharmacies. Your doctor will be able to comprehend what type of skin you have and what products will suit you best.

  • Excessive cleansing

    What’s with the over cleansing? We may become obsessed with cleansing to deal with acne but it actually aggravates the problem. Washing your face too much can remove the essential oil from your skin, drying it out and promoting it to produce too much oil, which can cause acne. It’s best to cleanse your face twice a day with a gentle, fragrance-free cleanser.

  • Not cleaning makeup brushes

    Brushes, brushes, brushes, they are easy to use for fast and quick makeup application. They are great. Right? But they are also one of the leading causes of acne. Gross, but true. They effectively attract oil, dirt, and grime if not cleaned regularly. So, make sure to soak these tools in warm water and shampoo them gently at least once in a week.

  • Reduce/Quit Smoking and Drinking Alcohol

    Smoking does not only cause cancer but can also lead to major breakouts on your skin, particularly non-inflamed blackheads and blocked pores. Smoking irritates the skin, causing it to produce more oil and possibly result in breakouts. Alcohol, on the other hand, steals the hydration away from your body and leaves you feeling dry, bloated and redder.

  • Reduce Intake of Extremely Spicy Food

    When you eat very spicy food, it irritates the skin by causing an imbalance to the pH level (of the skin), leading to breakouts. You should try eating mildly spicy and very spicy food in moderation.

  • Reduce Intake of Dairy Products if necessary

    Some researches have shown that the presence of hormone IGF 1 (insulin-like growth factor) which is found in milk can cause inflammation in humans. Milk can cause an insulin spike in humans and will force the production of IGF1 (in the liver). This will cause an excess production of sebum that will lead to an increased number of clogged pores and thus – acne.

Everybody enjoys the thought of a blemish free face and glowing skin at all times. If you take a little more care of yours, you could be enjoying the same!
