How to read and use nutrition facts
labels? Here's a guide

Good nutrition will always be of prime importance. It helps us stay healthy and allows our bodies to function properly. Therefore, when you can read and comprehend the nutrition facts thoroughly, you will be able to make better lifestyle choices. Good nutrition helps manage several conditions, such as diabetes, high blood pressure, certain cancers, heart disease, obesity, osteoporosis, etc. Also, if you are suffering from any health conditions or allergies, knowing all the nutrition facts beforehand will help you steer clear of the products you must not consume.

Reading the nutrition label is incredibly easy. So, what is a nutrition label? It is basically a complete profile of a food product. It usually lets you know in detail about the amount of calories, fat, saturated fat, trans fat, cholesterol, sodium, carbohydrates, fibre, sugar, protein, vitamin A, vitamin C, calcium, iron, and other nutrients in a particular product.

  • Serving Size

    Under the 'nutrition facts', you must first look for the serving size of the product. It will help you keep a track of all the calories, fats, and other nutrients that you will be consuming. Make sure you compare the amount of food written on the label to the amount you actually need because these amounts can vary.

  • %Daily Value

    This displays the quantity of the nutrients that are present in the food product. Let's take an example, an 8% of daily value (DV) of fibre means one serving of this food provides 8% of fibre, which a person must consume in a day.
    Now, you must always keep in mind that 5% or less DV is very little and 15% or more DV is too much. However, when it comes to sodium you can go below 5% and for fibre, you can go above 15%.

  • Calories

    The number of calories helps you understand the amount of energy you will gain from one serving of the food. The most common calorie consumption guidelines for adults is 2000 calories per day (approx.). Therefore, when you are making the purchase, you can calculate how much of it you would like to consume.

  • Fat, Saturated fat, and Trans Fat

    Always remember all fats are not equal. For example, Omega-3 fats found in fish are polyunsaturated and avocados contain monosaturated fats. These fats are healthy fats, which promote heart health. Therefore, you must try to consume less saturated and trans fats. For an upper daily limit, you must not consume more than 10% of these bad fats. So, for a 2000 calorie diet, it is 20 grams. However, it is important to keep the fats below 65 grams.

  • Cholesterol

    High cholesterol leads to several health problems. Therefore, to control your blood cholesterol, you must consume food with lower saturated and trans-fat. Your daily cholesterol intake must not go more than 300 milligrams per day. If a product claim to be cholesterol free, it must contain less than 2 milligrams of cholesterol, and also low saturated and trans-fat.

  • Sodium

    Your daily sodium intake must be less than 1500 milligrams or less than 1 teaspoon. Your maximum sodium intake can go up to 2300 milligrams and not more than that. If a product claims to be sodium free, it must contain less than 5 milligrams of sodium in each serving. Always look for products that have less than 360 milligrams of sodium in each serving.

  • Carbohydrates

    The number that represents carbohydrates is a total of sugar, starch, and fibre in one serving. It is mandatory for the manufacturers to list the sugar and fibre quantity, under carbohydrates. However, the starch quantity is optional. We need sugar and starch in our daily fuel to work both our brain and muscles. And fibre is a non-digestible carbohydrate, which is beneficial for your health. Therefore, your daily levels of carbohydrates must be around 300 grams.

  • Fibre

    If the food claims to be a 'source of fibre', it means, this product contains at least 2 grams of fibre. If it claims to be 'high source of fibre', it contains at least 4 grams of fibre, and if it says 'a very high source of fibre, it contains about 6 grams in it. You must consume at least 25 grams of fibre every day.

  • Sugar

    The total grams of sugar is a sum of both refined sugar and natural sugar. Therefore, next time if you see your breakfast cereal, which contains fruit with higher sugar compared to the plain variety, it is because of the natural sugar. And it is not a bad thing. Always make sure to choose products with natural sugars rather than the ones which contain refined sugars.

  • Protein

    You need amino acids to build and maintain a healthy body. Also, with the help of protein, you can feel fuller for longer. Adults require .8 grams of protein/kg of their body weight. Therefore, a person who weighs 70 kilos will require 56 grams (70*0.8) of protein each day.
