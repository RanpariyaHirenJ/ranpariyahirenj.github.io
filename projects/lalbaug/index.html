Lalbaugcha Raja

Lalbaugcha Raja is one of the biggest and most beloved Sarvajanik Ganpati in Mumbai. Lalbaug, a prominent locality in Mumbai, serves as the idolâ€™s home during the eleven day long Ganesh Chaturthi festival. Thereafter, it is immersed on the auspicious day of Anant Chaturdashi.

The Lalbaug Sarvajanik Ganeshostav Mandal which organises the festival was founded in 1934. The story goes like this: The bustling Peru Chawl was shut down by the government displacing hundreds of hawkers, fisherfolk, traders, little stall owners and bit sellers. Distraught at the thought of losing their livelihood, the vendors prayed to the divine king of benefaction, to give them a place to call their own.

Divinity smiled. With some serious efforts and support of the local authorities and prominent citizens, a new market came up in the year 1934. With their wish fulfilled, the vendors and fisherfolk were ecstatic. Their Raja had heard their prayers. Grateful for His intervention that granted them a permanent place under the sun, they decided to show their affection for Lord Ganesha by installing the first idol on the Ganesh Chaturthi day as a part of SarvajanikGaneshotsav or the public celebrations of the Ganesh festival. The day was September 12, 1934.

It was the beginning of what was later to become a phenomenon called 'Lalbaugcha Raja'.

Formed at the peak of the freedom struggle, the festival was introduced by the Indian freedom fighter and social worker Lokmanya Tilak to encourage social gatherings during the British era, as an act of rebellion. Since then, Lalbaugcha Raja has grown both in stature and popularity and attracts millions of people from all over the country year after year exemplifying the power of bhakti. Moreover, the King Lord is distictly secular, and this particular event is open to members of all castes and communities. It is believed that this Idol of Lord Ganesha is Navsacha Ganpati (which means the "one who fulfills all wishes"), which could also explain the massive, meandering queues one needs to navigate just to get a glimpse of Bappa.

The idol has been sculpted by Kambli family for over eight decades now and has seen a lot of innovative changes over the years. Lalbaughcha Raja was shown as the avatars of Lord Rama, Lord Vishnu and Lord Krishna. It was also given the face of Mahatma Gandhi during a year after Independence. For the past few years though, the idol has remained consistent in its appearance.
